2. What the App does

Animal Vault is a farm and livestock management application. It allows you to:

  • Create and manage farms and livestock inventory (animals, categories, batches).
  • Track purchases, expenses, health records, deaths, and sales.
  • Manage stock, procurement, inventory, and finances.
  • Receive AI-powered insights (for example, climate risk, health and mortality risk, financial summaries, and stock opportunities).
  • Use cloud sync only: you must sign in with a cloud account to use the App and sync your data. There is no offline-only or local-only mode.

Some features are available only to paid subscribers; free-tier use is described in Section 7.

3. Information you provide to us

We collect the information you choose to provide when you:

  • Install and use the App.
  • Create an account or sign in.
  • Enter data into the App (for example, animals, farms, expenses, orders, health records).
  • Contact us for support or feedback.

3.1 Account and contact details

If you create an account or sign in, we may collect:

  • Name (or display name).
  • Email address.
  • Authentication data (for example, hashed password or tokens from sign-in providers).

3.2 Farm and livestock data

To provide the core functionality we store data you enter, such as:

  • Farm names, locations (high-level, not necessarily GPS), and configuration.
  • Livestock information (species, groups/batches, counts, identifiers, status).
  • Transactions (purchases, expenses, sales, receivables, payables).
  • Health and husbandry records (treatments, vaccinations, deaths, notes).
  • Inventory, feed stocks, suppliers, and related details.

This data is primarily stored to provide you with a working farm ledger and analytics.

3.3 Financial information

Animal Vault is not a banking or payments app; we do not collect card numbers or bank-account credentials inside the App itself.

We may store:

  • Monetary amounts, currencies, prices, and totals you enter.
  • Optional references such as invoice numbers, purchase‑order IDs, and payment status.

This information is used only to show you reports, insights, and reminders about your own records.

3.4 Support and feedback

If you contact us (for example, by email), we may collect:

  • Your name and email address.
  • The content of your message and any attachments.

We use this solely to respond and improve the App.

4. Information from device permissions

The App may request access to certain device features. Granting these permissions is optional, but some features may not work or may be limited without them.

4.1 Location (approximate and precise)

When you grant location access, we may collect:

  • Approximate and/or precise device location (for example, via GPS, Wi‑Fi, or cell towers).

We use this to:

  • Show weather‑based climate risk alerts for your livestock.
  • Provide location-aware AI insights (for example, seasonal context, local climate patterns).

You can use the App without location permission, but some weather‑aware and AI features will be less accurate or unavailable.

4.2 Camera

When you grant camera access, we may process:

  • Images or video frames you capture while using in‑app camera features (for example, animal photos, receipts, tags, or barcodes).

We use this to:

  • Attach photos to animals, expenses, and other records.
  • Scan barcodes or tags for faster data entry.

We do not access your camera outside of the specific actions you initiate (for example, tapping “Take photo” or “Scan”).

4.3 Photos / media library

When you grant access to your photo library or similar storage:

  • We may read only the photos you explicitly select, to attach them to animals, expenses, or other records.
  • Where you choose, we may write new images or export files (for example, backups) into your library or storage location.

We do not otherwise scan or index your photo library.

4.4 Notifications

When you enable local or push notifications, we may process:

  • A device token (for push, if configured) and notification preferences.
  • Scheduling metadata (for example, due dates and reminder types).

We use this to send reminders such as:

  • Vaccination and health‑check reminders.
  • Feed and inventory alerts.
  • Pending payments, order due dates, and monthly expense reminders.

You can adjust notification settings in the App (where provided) and via your device’s system settings.

4.5 Biometrics (Face ID or similar)

When you enable biometric lock (for example, via Face ID or the local_auth plugin):

  • We receive from the operating system only the result of the biometric check (success/failure).
  • We do not receive or store your biometric template (face/print data); this is managed entirely by your device.

We use biometric results only to lock and unlock sensitive areas in the App (for example, financials or inventory views).

5. Automatically collected technical data

The App may collect limited technical information for diagnostics and analytics, such as:

  • Device model, operating system version, App version.
  • Basic performance and crash information (for example, via platform-provided crash reporting).

We use this to keep the App stable, secure, and up to date. We do not use this information to build marketing profiles or to track you across unrelated apps.

6. AI insights and your data

The App provides AI‑powered insights (for example, climate risk, mortality risk, financial summaries, and opportunities). These features work by sending structured prompts built from:

  • Your own farm, livestock, and financial data.
  • Optional context like location‑derived weather summaries.
  • System prompts that describe how the AI should respond.

We currently use third‑party AI providers (such as Google Gemini / Google Cloud AI). When you invoke an AI feature, we may send the necessary context to such providers so they can compute a response.

We:

  • Limit prompts to the minimum information required to generate the requested insight.
  • Use reputable providers that offer contractual data‑protection commitments.

However, AI outputs may contain errors or omissions. AI insights are not professional advice and should be confirmed with qualified veterinarians, agronomists, or financial advisors before making critical decisions.
